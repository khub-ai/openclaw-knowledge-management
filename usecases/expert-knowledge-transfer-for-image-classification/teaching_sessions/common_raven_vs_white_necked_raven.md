# Common Raven vs White-necked Raven: Identification Guide

**Source**: Sibley Guide to Birds; HBW Alive; Birds of Africa (Sinclair & Ryan)
**Feature group**: plumage patch + bill + range
**Attribute similarity (CUB-200-2011)**: 0.9572

## Overview
Both are large, all-black ravens with heavy bills. The White-necked Raven is an African and Middle Eastern species; within the CUB-200-2011 dataset, range cannot be assumed, so visual features must be used. The white neck base (visible when feathers are ruffled) and bill shape are the primary marks.

## Key Identification Features

### 1. White neck base
White-necked Raven has white bases to the feathers on the neck and upper back. This is usually hidden but becomes visible when the bird ruffles its feathers in wind, display, or when preening. Under the right conditions this white patch is unmistakable; when feathers are sleeked down, the bird looks all-black. Look for any white visible on the neck or upper mantle.

### 2. Bill shape and size
White-necked Raven has a shorter, deeper bill with a more strongly arched, curved culmen — the ridge of the upper mandible curves down steeply toward the tip. The Common Raven's bill is longer and the culmen has a gentler curve. Both bills are very heavy, but the depth-to-length ratio differs: White-necked has a deeper, stubbier bill.

### 3. Overall size
White-necked Raven is noticeably smaller than Common Raven — roughly the size of a large crow, whereas Common Raven is substantially larger (comparable to a Red-tailed Hawk in bulk). When size reference is available this is useful.

### 4. Tail shape in flight
Common Raven has a distinctive long, wedge-shaped (diamond-pointed) tail, visible in soaring flight. White-necked Raven has a shorter, more rounded tail.

### 5. Throat hackles
Common Raven has prominent, elongated throat hackles (pointed feathers on the throat) that it fans out during calling. White-necked Raven has shorter, less dramatic hackles.

## In the Field: Decision Rules
- If any white is visible on the neck or upper back, it is a White-necked Raven.
- If the tail is long and distinctly wedge-shaped in soaring flight, it is a Common Raven.
- If the bill has a very strongly arched, deep profile (stubby appearance), lean toward White-necked Raven.
- If the bird is very large (noticeably larger than a crow) with a long wedge tail, it is a Common Raven.
- White neck feather bases visible when bird ruffles: White-necked Raven.

## Female / Immature / Non-breeding
Both sexes identical in both species. Juveniles show duller, brownish-black plumage in both species; white neck base of juvenile White-necked Raven is present but may be less white than adults.

## Voice
Common Raven: deep, hollow "kronk" or "pruk." White-necked Raven: higher-pitched, more crow-like calls. Voice is a secondary feature; not reliable from photographs.

## Quick Reference
| Feature | Common Raven | White-necked Raven |
|---|---|---|
| Size | Very large (22–27 in) | Large (19–20 in) |
| White neck base | Absent | Present (when ruffled) |
| Bill profile | Long, gently curved | Shorter, deeply arched |
| Tail | Long wedge-shaped | Shorter, rounded |
| Throat hackles | Prominent | Less developed |
| Range | Holarctic | Africa, Middle East |
