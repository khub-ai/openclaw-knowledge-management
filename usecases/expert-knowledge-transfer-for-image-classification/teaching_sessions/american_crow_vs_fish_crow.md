# American Crow vs Fish Crow: Identification Guide

**Source**: All About Birds (Cornell Lab of Ornithology); Sibley Guide to Birds
**Feature group**: size + voice
**Attribute similarity (CUB-200-2011)**: 0.9960 (most similar pair in dataset)

## Overview
American Crow and Fish Crow are nearly identical visually — both are large, all-black corvids. The Fish Crow is slightly smaller and slimmer, but size is unreliable without direct comparison. Voice is the most reliable field mark. Where the two species overlap (eastern and southeastern US), learning the call is essential.

## Key Identification Features

### 1. Voice (most reliable)
The Fish Crow gives a nasal, high-pitched double note: "uh-uh" or "nyah" — often described as sounding like a crow with a cold. The American Crow gives a full, deep "caw" or "caw-caw-caw." When in doubt, try to get the bird to call. A bird giving the nasal "uh-uh" is almost certainly a Fish Crow.

### 2. Size and build
Fish Crow is noticeably smaller than American Crow — roughly Robin-sized vs. a large crow. It also appears slimmer and more slender-billed. However, size is highly unreliable in isolation; juveniles and females of each species overlap. Only useful when direct comparison is possible (mixed flocks).

### 3. Tail shape in flight
Fish Crow has a shorter, more squared-off tail. American Crow shows a more rounded tail fan when the tail is spread in flight. This is subtle and requires practice.

### 4. Wingbeat
Fish Crow's wingbeat is slightly faster and has a more buoyant, bouncy quality compared to the heavier, more deliberate flap of the American Crow.

### 5. Bill
Fish Crow has a slightly more slender, shorter bill relative to its head size. American Crow's bill appears thicker and longer proportionally. This is a supporting feature, not diagnostic.

## In the Field: Decision Rules
- If the call is a nasal "uh-uh" or "nyah," it is a Fish Crow.
- If the call is a full, deep "caw," it is an American Crow.
- If the bird is noticeably smaller than nearby American Crows in a mixed flock, consider Fish Crow.
- If the tail appears squared off in flight (not rounded), lean toward Fish Crow.
- If you cannot hear the call and cannot compare size, the bird should be recorded as "Crow sp." — visual-only identification is often not possible.

## Female / Immature / Non-breeding
Both species are all black year-round; sexes are identical in appearance. Juvenile Fish Crows may show slightly brownish wash but this is rarely useful in the field.

## Voice
Diagnostic. Fish Crow: nasal "uh-uh." American Crow: deep "caw." This is the primary identification feature.

## Quick Reference
| Feature | American Crow | Fish Crow |
|---|---|---|
| Size | Large (17–21 in) | Medium (14–16 in) |
| Call | Deep "caw" | Nasal "uh-uh" |
| Tail in flight | Rounded fan | Shorter, squared |
| Bill | Thick, heavy | Slimmer, shorter |
| Wingbeat | Heavy, deliberate | Faster, more buoyant |
| Range | Widespread N. America | Coastal/southeastern US |
